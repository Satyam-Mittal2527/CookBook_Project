Nepali Thali Recipe!

Ingredients

    For Masu (Meat Curry):

  • 500g meat (chicken, mutton, or pork)
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil or ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish


    • For Saag (Green Leafy Vegetables):

  • 500g green leafy vegetables (spinach, mustard greens, or swiss chard)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste


    • For Aalu Tareko (Fried Potatoes):

  • 3-4 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste


    • For Aachar (Pickle):

  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 1 tbsp mustard oil
  • Salt to taste


    • For Dal (Lentil Soup):

  • 1 cup lentils (yellow or red)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish


    • For Gundruk (Fermented Leafy Greens):

  • 200g gundruk
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

    ⤷Prepare the Masu (Meat Curry):

  1. In a large pot, heat oil or ghee and add chopped onions, garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
  2. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes are soft.
  3. Add the meat and cook until it is browned and cooked through. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  4. Add garam masala and mix well. Cook for an additional 5-10 minutes. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

    ⤷Prepare the Saag (Green Leafy Vegetables):

  1. In a pan, heat oil or ghee and add mustard seeds. Let them splutter.
  2. Add minced garlic and chopped green chili. Sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add the green leafy vegetables and salt. Cook until the vegetables are wilted and tender.

    ⤷Prepare the Aalu Tareko (Fried Potatoes):

  1. In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Let them splutter.
  2. Add the diced potatoes, turmeric powder, and salt. Fry until the potatoes are golden and crispy.

    ⤷Prepare the Aachar (Pickle):

  1. In a bowl, combine chopped tomatoes, cucumber, green chilies, turmeric powder, and salt.
  2. In a small pan, heat mustard oil and add fenugreek seeds. Let them splutter.
  3. Pour the hot mustard oil and fenugreek seeds over the tomato mixture. Mix well.

    ⤷Prepare the Dal (Lentil Soup):

  1. Rinse the lentils thoroughly and soak them in water for about 30 minutes.
  2. In a large pot, add the soaked lentils and 4 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer.
  3. In a separate pan, heat oil or ghee and add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and asafoetida. Let them splutter.
  4. Add chopped onions, garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes are soft.
  6. Pour this mixture into the simmering lentils and mix well. Cook for an additional 10-15 minutes or until the lentils are fully cooked.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

    ⤷Prepare the Gundruk (Fermented Leafy Greens):

  1. In a pan, heat oil or ghee and add chopped onions, garlic, and green chili. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
  2. Add chopped tomatoes and salt. Cook until the tomatoes are soft.
  3. Add the gundruk and mix well. Cook for an additional 5-10 minutes.
