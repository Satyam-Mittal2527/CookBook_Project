Flavours of the Himalayas!

Nepali Thakali

 A traditional Nepali meal consisting of rice, lentil  soup, vegetable curry, and pickles.
Sel Roti

 A traditional homemade, sweet, ring-shaped rice  bread/doughnut.
Yomari

 A steamed dumpling made of rice flour and filled with  sweet substances like molasses or sesame seeds.
Dhido

 A traditional Nepali food made from buckwheat or  millet flour, served with various side dishes.
Gorkhali Lamb

 A spicy lamb curry with vegetables and spices.
Juju Dhau

 A special type of yogurt from Bhaktapur, known for its   rich and creamy texture.
Chatamari

 A kind of Nepali pizza made from rice flour and topped  with minced meat, eggs, and vegetables.
Chicken Choila

 A spicy grilled chicken salad mixed with spices and  herbs.
Momo

 Nepali dumplings filled with meat or vegetables,  served with a spicy dipping sauce.
Thukpa

 A noodle soup with meat, vegetables, and spices.
Kheer

 A rice pudding made with milk, sugar, and spices.
Sukuti

 Dried meat seasoned with spices, often served as a  snack or appetizer.
Aloo Tama

 A curry made with potatoes and bamboo shoots,  seasoned with spices.
Kwati

 A mixed bean soup, traditionally consumed during the  festival of Janai Purnima.
Ghonghi

 River snails cooked with spices, a delicacy in certain  regions of Nepal.
Sikarni

 A sweet yogurt dessert, flavored with spices like  cardamom and saffron.
Nepali Bhuttan

 Fried goat intestines, a popular snack or appetizer.
Chhaang

 A traditional fermented rice or barley beverage  popular in the Himalayas, symbolizing hospitality and  community.
