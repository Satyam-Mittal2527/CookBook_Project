Puttu

A steamed rice cake layered with coconut.

Ingredients: Rice flour, Grated coconut, Water, Salt.

Steps:

  1. Take rice flour in a bowl and add a pinch of salt.
  2. Sprinkle water gradually and mix until the flour turns moist and crumbly.
  3. Layer a puttu maker with alternating layers of grated coconut and moistened rice flour.
  4. Steam for 8-10 minutes until cooked through.
  5. Serve hot with banana, sugar, or kadala curry.

Pongal

A savory rice and lentil dish.

Ingredients: Rice, Moong dal, Ghee, Black pepper, Cashews.

Steps:

  1. Dry roast the moong dal for a few minutes until aromatic.
  2. Wash and cook the rice and dal together with water in a pressure cooker for 3-4 whistles.
  3. In a pan, heat ghee and fry cumin seeds, black pepper, and cashews.
  4. Mix the tempering into the cooked rice and dal mixture.
  5. Serve hot with coconut chutney or sambar.

Payasam

A sweet pudding made with milk, rice, and jaggery.

Ingredients: Milk, Rice, Jaggery, Cardamom, Cashews.

Steps:

  1. Boil the milk in a pan.
  2. Add rice and cook until the rice becomes soft.
  3. Stir in jaggery, cardamom, and cashews.
  4. Let it simmer for a few minutes until it thickens.
  5. Serve warm or chilled.

Paniyaram

A crispy and soft snack made from fermented rice and lentil batter.

Ingredients: Rice, Urad dal, Green chilies, Curry leaves.

Steps:

  1. Soak rice and urad dal overnight and grind into a smooth batter.
  2. Ferment the batter for 8-10 hours.
  3. Heat a paniyaram pan, add a little oil in each cavity.
  4. Pour the batter into the pan and cook until golden brown on both sides.
  5. Serve hot with chutney or sambar.

Kuttu

A nutritious dish made with vegetables and lentils.

Ingredients: Mixed vegetables, Chana dal, Coconut, Spices.

Steps:

  1. Soak chana dal for 2-3 hours and cook until soft.
  2. Chop vegetables and cook them with spices in a pan.
  3. Mix the cooked dal and vegetables, then add grated coconut.
  4. Stir well and cook for 5 more minutes.
  5. Serve hot with chapati or rice.

Chettinad Chicken

A spicy and flavorful chicken dish from Chettinad cuisine.

Ingredients: Chicken, Coconut, Black pepper, Spices.

Steps:

  1. Grind coconut, black pepper, and spices into a fine paste.
  2. Fry onions, ginger, garlic, and tomatoes in oil.
  3. Add the chicken pieces and cook until browned.
  4. Mix in the ground paste, water, and cook until chicken is tender.
  5. Serve hot with rice or paratha.

Coconut Chutney

A tangy and spicy chutney made from fresh coconut.

Ingredients: Grated coconut, Green chilies, Ginger, Mustard seeds, Curry leaves.

Steps:

  1. In a blender, combine grated coconut, green chilies, ginger, and a little water to form a smooth paste.
  2. In a small pan, heat oil and add mustard seeds, curry leaves, and dry red chilies. Let them splutter.
  3. Pour the tempering over the chutney and mix well.
  4. Serve with idli, dosa, or any other South Indian dish.

Hyderabadi Biryani

A rich and aromatic biryani from Hyderabad.

Ingredients: Basmati rice, Meat, Saffron, Spices.

Steps:

  1. Marinate meat with yogurt, spices, and saffron.
  2. Cook rice separately until 70% done.
  3. Layer the marinated meat and partially cooked rice in a pot and seal it.
  4. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes to let the flavors blend.
  5. Serve hot with raita or salad.

Avial

A mixed vegetable dish with coconut and yogurt.

Ingredients: Mixed vegetables, Coconut, Yogurt, Curry leaves.

Steps:

  1. Boil the mixed vegetables until tender but firm.
  2. Grind coconut and green chilies into a smooth paste.
  3. Mix the coconut paste with yogurt and add it to the boiled vegetables.
  4. Cook for a few minutes and add curry leaves for flavor.
  5. Serve hot with rice or roti.

Appam

Soft, lacy rice pancakes often paired with coconut milk or stew.

Ingredients: Rice, Coconut milk, Yeast, Sugar.

Steps:

  1. Soak rice overnight and grind with coconut milk into a smooth batter.
  2. Ferment the batter for 6-8 hours.
  3. Heat a non-stick pan and pour a ladle of batter, swirl to form a lacy edge.
  4. Cook until the center is soft and the edges are crispy.
  5. Serve hot with stew or coconut milk.

Filter Coffee

A traditional South Indian coffee made with a metal filter.

Ingredients: Coffee powder, Hot water, Boiled milk, Sugar.

Steps:

  1. Place coffee powder in the top compartment of a South Indian filter.
  2. Pour hot water over the coffee powder and let it drip through into the bottom compartment.
  3. Boil milk and mix it with the brewed coffee in a cup.
  4. Add sugar to taste and stir well.
  5. Serve hot in a traditional tumbler or davara.
